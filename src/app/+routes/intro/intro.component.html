Considerations

Goal:

Create an extensible mobile image gallery.

Connecting the Dots:

The opportunity lies within not only creating a mobile image gallery but also offers an ergonomic implementation.

Process Diagram:

Why Angular:

Angular is a batteries included mobile/web framework and it's my personal weapon of choice!

For me, Angular affords the opportunity to work quickly and efficiently from its robust features like:

  • Angular CLI
  • Animations
  • Material Design
  • Templating
  • Routing

How Extensible:

I have created a single component under the name gxe-gallery. The gxe-gallery component takes a single Input() [galleryItems]="data" to render the entire experience.

Reflections:

As a developer, I got too excited and jumped to coding too quickly. In hindsight, I should have planned and outlined more of the nuance shown in the design mocks. In particular, one of the big problems was in the grid layout borders. My animations math wasn't great and adding border to the equation made my animation code verbose.

In the future I would like to support:

  • Desktop
  • Better IOS support
  • Tests
  • Pan and flick interactions
  • Tool bar Animations

Goal:

Create an Image Gallery and enhanced with motion design

Connecting the Dots:

The opportunity lies within the gap between designer and developer. Take an unclear mock up; turn out a thoughtful interaction with buttery smooth animations.

Justifications:

The interaction and motion design we're explicitly kept simple, only using Material Design and Google Photos as design precedence.

Motion:

Material design's motion is meticulously justified I tried to achieve motion in the UI that was:

  • Responsive
  • Natural
  • Aware
  • Intentional

Interaction:

Google Photo's Interaction Design is familiar to it's users. And therefore, I leveraged many of the interaction from it.

Open and Close:

Paginate, Tap, & Swipe

Reflections:

In hindsight, I wish I would have explored motion and interaction more. I was nervous to explore because the brief lacked context and time constraint.

In the future I would like to support:

  • Original Motion
  • Animation and functionality in Gallery Detail toolbars
  • A close interaction with vertical pan and physics.
